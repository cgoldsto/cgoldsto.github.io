Logos and Favicon

Black and White Version #1

This version uses a medium gray background to maximize utility. The background color provides sufficient contrast with both black and white. (I used the Coolors color contrast checker to verify.) This means the logo could work on both dark and light pages.

Reversed Black and White Version

This version uses the inverses of the first logo's colors. The background colors for the first two logos are inverses of each other; they just happen to look similar since they are both medium shades of gray.

Black and White Version - No Background

This version uses the same colors for the star and letters as logo #1. However, this version does not include the background square. This version could work well on a page with a medium-saturation background color, or a page with a color similar to (but slightly different) from logo #1's medium gray.

Color Version

I used orange and red because these colors evoke the concepts of energy and dynamic content. Honestly, I struggled with choosing a color palette. The Adobe Color wheel was very helpful for choosing colors that complement each other. However, I had trouble choosing shades of red and orange that provide sufficient color contrast. In the future, I want to fine-tune the color scheme. In particular, I want to find a bolder shade of red that still contrasts with the orange star, but also feels more energetic than the current dark red color.

Favicon

For the favicon, I decided to use the orange star from the logo. (You will also see the favicon in the tab icon of your browser.) The star is simple and recognizable.
